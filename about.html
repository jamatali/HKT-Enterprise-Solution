As the pressure to the diaphragm actuator increases, the stem moves downward causing the cam to rotate. The rotating cam causes the beam to pivot and moves the flapper to restrict the nozzle. The increased nozzle pressure moves the relay diaphragm assembly to open the valve in the relay. This increases the output pressure. As the output pressure increases, the bellow expands, pivots the beam and flapper slightly away from the nozzle. The transmitter is now in equilibrium with the transmitter output and proportional to the valve stem position. When the pressure to the diaphragm actuator decreases, the stem moves upward.
